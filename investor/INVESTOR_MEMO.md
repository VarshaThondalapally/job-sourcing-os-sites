# Investor Memo: Job Sourcing OS

## Thesis

The hiring market is shifting from search to routing.

The winner is not the tool that finds the most jobs. The winner is the system that proves which opportunities are alive, reachable, and worth a specific packet.

## Wedge

Start with high-agency candidates targeting hard-to-reach AI, FDE, product, and founding roles.

These users feel the pain sharply because:

- postings are fragmented across ATS, LinkedIn, YC, HN, company pages, and fresh-feed tools
- many postings are stale or mirrored
- generic applications are easy to ignore
- route quality matters more than volume
- blockers such as sponsorship, location, seniority, and domain fit can invalidate otherwise strong roles

## Why Now

AI has made applications cheaper to produce.

That creates a second-order problem:

```text
more applications
+ more generic AI writing
+ more noisy applicant pools
= less human signal
```

The scarce layer becomes:

- active-role verification
- human route discovery
- specific proof fit
- quality control
- follow-up discipline
- feedback from outcomes

Job extraction is only Layer 3.

The higher-value product lives in verification, route intelligence, fit proof, blocker analysis, outreach quality, response monitoring, and learning from outcomes.

## Risks

### Overbuilding Before Response Data

The system can become too elaborate before proving response-rate lift.

Mitigation:

Start with the smallest loop that measures route quality and human replies.

### Generic Outreach Relapse

The product can regress into writing polished but interchangeable outreach.

Mitigation:

Force the one-sentence fit gate, person-route confidence A/B/C/D, and four-company test before any message is approved.

### Source Fragility

Job sources change, block access, or present stale data.

Mitigation:

Separate discovery from truth. Official source verification remains mandatory.

### Privacy And Sensitive Claims

Candidates may expose confidential employer details or sensitive work-authorization context.

Mitigation:

Use evidence labels, human approval gates, and explicit confidentiality checks.

### Low Early Sample Size

The feedback loop cannot produce strong conclusions without enough reply data.

Mitigation:

Label early conclusions as low-confidence and keep source-route experiments structured.
